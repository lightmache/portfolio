Systems & Compliance Design (APL Work)

Work conducted in a high-constraint research environment focused on structured workflows, decision systems, and controlled data transformation pipelines. Artifacts are reconstructed for portfolio clarity.

Federally funded research environment · Systems UX · Constraint-driven design

Geospatial Workflow System

Designed a structured workflow for transforming static geospatial inputs into interactive, navigable artifacts through a controlled multi-stage processing pipeline.

Static spatial inputs → structured transformation → interactive output artifact

The system focused on improving how spatial data was organized, processed, and presented for downstream interpretation.

Decision Intake & Risk Evaluation System

Designed a structured intake system for evaluating high-constraint inputs through a multi-stage review process.

Risk signals → structured summary → review-ready decision state

The interface reduced cognitive load by consolidating multi-variable risk inputs into a single structured decision view.

Conditional logic supported progressive disclosure, reducing unnecessary input complexity while preserving required evaluation fields.

Design Constraints

Both systems were designed under strict constraints:

• Structured, auditable workflows
• Controlled information transformation pipelines
• Reduction of cognitive load in high-complexity environments

Outcome

Across both systems, the primary impact was improved clarity of structured workflows and reduced procedural friction in high-constraint environments.

The work emphasized transforming complex inputs into structured, reviewable outputs that supported consistent downstream interpretation.
