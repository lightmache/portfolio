Case Study: Working Across Data Silos

Digitizing and Integrating High-Stakes Inspections in Critical Infrastructure Systems

The Challenge: Breaking Physical & Data Silos in Long-Term Operations

Managing critical infrastructure involves complex dependencies across electrical, civil, and mechanical components. Historically, inspections relied on static, paper-based reporting.

While these reports contained high-value technical data, the information was trapped in silos. This created a “translation gap” where manual observations were disconnected from enterprise systems, making auditable lifecycle tracking and real-time risk assessment difficult.

Augmented Reality (AR) feasibility study comparing digital capture vs traditional paper-based inspection workflows.

The Solution: From Augmented Reality to Universal Field Integration

The work began by leading digital pilot studies using Augmented Reality (AR) to test the feasibility of replacing paper methods.

As shown in the chart above, these studies provided critical data demonstrating that digital capture could perform competitively with traditional workflows in terms of task completion time, while significantly improving data structure and traceability.

This system enabled structured capture of field observations directly into digital workflows, reducing fragmentation between inspection teams and enterprise asset management systems.

By moving from static documentation to integrated digital capture, inspection data became actionable across the full lifecycle of infrastructure management.
